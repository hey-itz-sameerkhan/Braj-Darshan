Govardhan Hills

Govardhan Hill, also known as Giri Raj, is a revered site in Hindu mythology and is deeply associated with Lord Krishna. Located about 22 km from Vrindavan, this sandstone hill stretches across 8 km and stands 80 feet tall. It is considered a natural manifestation of Lord Krishna, and devotees believe that worshipping the hill is equivalent to worshipping Krishna himself.

The hill is dotted with temples, lakes, and historical monuments. Notable sites include the Sandstone Monument, Kusum Sarovar Lake, Giriraj Temple, and Radha Kund Temple. The terrain is rugged, with pathways for devotees performing the 23 km parikrama.
üìçLocation: Parikrama Marg, Govardhan
